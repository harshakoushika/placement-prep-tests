## Puzzle 1: Three Switches Problem

### Problem

You are outside a room with three switches. Inside the room there is a bulb. Only one switch controls the bulb.

You can toggle the switches as many times as you want, but you can enter the room only once. How will you determine which switch controls the bulb?

### Solution

1. Turn on switch 1 and leave it on for a a few minutes.
2. Turn it off and turn on switch 2.
3. Enter the room.
4. If the bulb is **on**, switch 2 controls it.
5. If the bulb is **off but warm**, switch 1 controls it.
6. If the bulb is **off and cold**, switch 3 controls it.

### Explanation

The heat of the bulb helps identify the switch that was previously turned on.

## Puzzle 2: The Missing Dollar Puzzle

### Problem

Three friends check into a hotel room costing $30. Each pays $10.

Later the manager realizes the room actually costs $25 and gives the bellboy $5 to return.
The bellboy keeps $2 and gives $1 back to each friend.

Now each friend paid $9 (total $27).
The bellboy kept $2.

$27 + $2 = $29.
Where did the missing dollar go?

### Solution

There is **no missing dollar**.

### Explanation

The $27 already includes the $2 kept by the bellboy.

Actual distribution:

* Hotel: $25
* Bellboy: $2
* Returned to friends: $3

Total = $30.

## Puzzle 3: Two Doors and Two Guards

### Problem

You are at a crossroads with two doors:

* One leads to freedom
* One leads to danger

There are two guards:

* One always tells the truth
* One always lies

You can ask **only one question to one guard**.
What should you ask?

### Solution

Ask:
"If I asked the other guard which door leads to freedom, what would he say?"

Then choose the **opposite door**.

### Explanation

Both the liar and truth-teller will point to the wrong door.

## Puzzle 4: Clock Angle Problem

### Problem

What is the angle between the hour hand and minute hand at **3:15**?

### Solution

Formula:

Angle = |30H − 5.5M|

H = 3
M = 15

Angle = |30 × 3 − 5.5 × 15|
Angle = |90 − 82.5|
Angle = **7.5°**

## Puzzle 6: Farmer, Fox, Chicken, Grain

### Problem

A farmer must cross a river with:

* a fox
* a chicken
* a bag of grain

The boat can carry only the farmer and one item.

Rules:

* Fox cannot stay with chicken.
* Chicken cannot stay with grain.

### Solution

1. Take chicken across.
2. Return alone.
3. Take fox across.
4. Bring chicken back.
5. Take grain across.
6. Return alone.
7. Take chicken across.

## Puzzle 7: The Bridge Crossing Problem

### Problem

Four people must cross a bridge at night with one torch.

Times:

* A = 1 minute
* B = 2 minutes
* C = 7 minutes
* D = 10 minutes

Only two people can cross at a time.

### Solution

1. A + B cross → 2
2. A returns → 1
3. C + D cross → 10
4. B returns → 2
5. A + B cross → 2

Total = **17 minutes**

## Puzzle 8: Water Jug Problem

### Problem

You have a **5L jug** and a **3L jug**.

Measure exactly **4 liters**.

### Solution

1. Fill 5L jug.
2. Pour into 3L jug → 2L remains.
3. Empty 3L jug.
4. Pour remaining 2L into 3L jug.
5. Fill 5L jug again.
6. Pour into 3L jug until full → 4L remains.

## Puzzle 9: Light Bulb Puzzle

### Problem

A man lives on the 10th floor.

Every day he takes the elevator down.
When returning:

* On rainy days → goes directly to floor 10
* On sunny days → stops at floor 7 and walks.

Why?

### Solution

He is **too short to reach the button for floor 10** and can only reach floor 7.

When raining, he uses his **umbrella to press the 10th-floor button**.

## Puzzle 10: Monty Hall Problem

### Problem

You choose one of three doors.
Behind one is a car; behind the others are goats.

The host opens one goat door and asks if you want to switch.

Should you switch?

### Solution

Yes.

### Explanation

Switching gives a **2/3 probability of winning**, while staying gives **1/3 probability**.
